# Tickets

The Tickets section allows you to manage and track support requests from users interacting with your AI assistants. This feature helps you identify issues, respond to user inquiries, and improve your assistants over time.

## Overview

The Tickets page displays a list of all support tickets generated from user interactions with your assistants. Each ticket represents a user request that requires attention from your team.

## Features

### Viewing Tickets

The main Tickets page displays a list of all tickets with key information:

- Ticket ID
- Creation date and time
- User information
- Status (Open, In Progress, Closed)
- Priority level
- Brief description of the issue

Tickets are typically sorted with the most recent or highest priority tickets at the top.

### Filtering Tickets

You can filter tickets based on various criteria:

1. **Status Filter**: Use the status dropdown to view tickets by their current status:
   - All Tickets
   - Open Tickets
   - In Progress Tickets
   - Closed Tickets

2. **Date Range**: Filter tickets by when they were created:
   - Click the date range selector
   - Choose your desired start and end dates
   - The ticket list will update to show only tickets created within that period

### Searching Tickets

To find specific tickets:

1. Use the search bar at the top of the ticket list
2. Enter keywords, ticket IDs, or user information
3. The list will automatically filter to show matching tickets

### Pagination

When you have many tickets, they are organized into pages:

- Navigate between pages using the pagination controls at the bottom of the list
- The current page number is highlighted
- You can jump to a specific page by clicking its number

### Managing Tickets

To manage a ticket:

1. Click on a ticket in the list to view its details
2. From the ticket details page, you can:
   - View the complete conversation history related to the ticket
   - Update the ticket status (Open, In Progress, Closed)
   - Assign the ticket to a team member
   - Add internal notes visible only to your team
   - Respond directly to the user

### User Roles and Permissions

Access to tickets depends on your role in the system:

- **Regular Users**: Can view and manage tickets related to their own assistants
- **Administrators**: Can view and manage all tickets across the organization

## Best Practices

- Regularly review open tickets to ensure timely responses to user inquiries
- Use ticket data to identify common issues and improve your assistants
- Maintain clear communication with users by providing regular updates on their tickets
- Close tickets only when the issue has been fully resolved
- Use internal notes to document troubleshooting steps or relevant information for your team
